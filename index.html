"Fireflies, or lightning bugs, aren’t actually flies at all – for that matter, they’re not even really bugs, either. In fact, from a biological standpoint, they’re part of the beetle family. Science aside, these beautiful insects come out once dusk begins in the summertime, and can be seen lighting up the night in many areas of the world."

"In China, it was believed fireflies were the result of burning gras"

Interestingly, not all fireflies light up. Melissa Breyer of Mother Nature Network says, "California has perfect weather, palm trees, and stellar food. But alas, it doesn’t have fireflies. Actually, let us restate that: it doesn’t have fireflies that light up. Of the more than 2,000 species of fireflies, only some come equipped with the ability to glow: the ones that are able to don’t generally live in the West."
